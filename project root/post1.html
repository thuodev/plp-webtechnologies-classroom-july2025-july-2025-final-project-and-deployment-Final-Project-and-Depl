My Journey at PLP

Published: September 2025

Joining the Power Learn Project (PLP) web development track was one of the best decisions I have made so far. At first, I had no idea what to expect — just excitement and a bit of nervousness. The first few weeks were about grasping the basics of HTML and CSS, which felt like learning a new language. Soon after, we moved into JavaScript, and that’s when things got even more interesting.

One of my biggest wins was understanding how all the pieces fit together — semantic HTML, responsive layouts with CSS, and interactivity with JavaScript. I built small projects along the way, tested ideas, and got better at debugging.

The highlight was the final project where I had to bring everything together in a multi-page, responsive website and deploy it. Seeing my site live on the internet for the first time gave me a huge confidence boost. This experience taught me that consistency matters more than speed — showing up daily to code slowly built my skills.

PLP not only gave me technical skills but also connected me to a community of learners who are passionate about tech and growth. I’m grateful for the mentorship and support that kept me motivated along the way.
